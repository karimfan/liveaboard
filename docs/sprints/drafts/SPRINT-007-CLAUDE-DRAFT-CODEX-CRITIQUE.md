# Codex Critique of `SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md`

## Findings

1. **The draft does not incorporate the binding Phase 4 scope cut for Site Director UX, and that leaks into both the content and the recommendation.** The intent explicitly says the Site Director chrome variant is dropped from Sprint 007, but the draft still treats it as one of the seven required domains, includes a full Site Director use case, scores options on "Site Director chrome simplicity," and adds a dedicated Site Director section at the end. This is not just extra content; it materially biases the recommendation toward Option A on role-gating grounds that are now out of scope. See [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:13), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:43), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:475), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:520).

2. **The inventory model is outdated and contradicts the planner’s confirmed answer that per-boat stock is quantity-based, not checkbox-based.** Phase 4 refinement #3 makes quantity tracking binding, but the draft continues to frame the boat-level catalog as "stocked items" and explicitly recommends checkboxes in the open questions section. That breaks a core IA requirement because quantity management is much heavier than availability toggling: it needs numeric inputs, low-stock states, and audit-friendly scanning. Option A’s boat detail, Option B’s catalog tab, and the Sprint 008 open-question framing all need to be reworked around a quantity grid. See [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:138), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:154), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:285), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:559).

3. **Option B’s IA mixes boat-scoped and trip-scoped work into one tab set, which makes the object model muddy and creates avoidable first-run friction.** `Manifest` and much of `Crew` are trip workflows, not boat workflows, yet they are promoted to persistent tabs inside a boat workspace. That means the user is asked to think "I am editing Gaia Love" while actually performing actions on a specific trip. It also creates the exact first-run problem the draft later admits: before a boat exists there is no primary workspace, and after a boat exists the route still has to explain whether the user is looking at the boat, the next trip, or trip history. See [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:226), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:286), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:293), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:313).

4. **Option C under-specifies how non-trip admin work remains discoverable, and the `⋯ Manage` pattern weakens IA clarity for an MVP that is still configuration-heavy.** The draft itself notes that Catalog, Settings, and Reports are hidden, but it understates the impact. In this product state, admins are not mostly editing trips yet; they are still standing up org settings, boats, catalog, users, and inventory. Hiding four of those surfaces behind a secondary menu makes first-run worse and makes role/routing boundaries less legible than Option A’s explicit sections. The proposal reads more like a mature optimization for active operators than an MVP admin foundation. See [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:343), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:364), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:405), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:436).

5. **The recommendation is directionally reasonable, but some of the supporting criteria are no longer the right ones.** Option A is still the pragmatic favorite, but the rationale leans too heavily on Site Director chrome simplicity and on a stale catalog model. With the Phase 4 refinements applied, the stronger reasons are: clean mapping of the seven admin domains, best support for quantity-heavy per-boat inventory, best first-run checklist, and cleanest fit with the existing dashboard shell. The recommendation should be reframed around those points instead of role-gating convenience. See [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:488), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:496).

6. **`DESIGN.md` compliance is asserted more than demonstrated, especially around density and visual tone.** The token preamble is correct, but the actual options do not say enough about how the chosen density behaves in practice for quantity grids, setup checklists, and split-pane detail work. The use of emoji/icon-heavy wireframes also pulls slightly away from the "serious, competent, operational" tone in `DESIGN.md`; the final merged doc should describe component behavior and hierarchy more than decorative nav glyphs. This is not a blocker, but the current draft is stronger on token recital than on operational UI discipline. See [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:47), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:77), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:221), [SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md](/Users/karim/src/liveaboard/docs/sprints/drafts/SPRINT-007-CLAUDE-DRAFT.md:348).

## What To Keep

- Option A is the right default recommendation.
- The first-run checklist in Option A is strong and should survive merge.
- The critique of Option B’s chicken-and-egg onboarding issue is accurate.
- The caution that Option C becomes attractive later if scheduling dominates is sound.

## Merge Guidance

- Remove Site Director UX from the required surface area, the matrix, and the recommendation criteria for Sprint 007.
- Replace all per-boat "stocked items" language with per-boat quantity management.
- Tighten Option A around a real `Overview` control-tower screen plus boat-detail inventory grids.
- Keep Option B and C as contrasts, but sharpen why they are worse for first-run admin setup rather than only why they cost more to implement.
